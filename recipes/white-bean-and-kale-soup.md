# White Bean and Kale Soup with Crispy Garlic

## Ingredients

- dried white beans - 1 1/2 cups, soaked overnight and drained :: id=beans
- extra-virgin olive oil - 3 tbsp, plus more for finishing
- onion - 1, diced
- carrot - 1, diced
- celery stalk - 1, diced
- garlic - 4 cloves, thinly sliced :: id=garlic
- smoked paprika - 1 tsp
- lacinato kale - 1 bunch, stemmed and roughly chopped
- vegetable broth - 5 cups
- salt and freshly ground black pepper
- crusty bread - for serving

## Steps

1. In a large pot, heat 2 tbsp of [[extra-virgin olive oil]] over medium heat. Add the [[onion]], [[carrot]], and [[celery stalk]] and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 8 minutes.
2. Add half the [[garlic]] and the [[smoked paprika]], stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add the [[beans]] and [[vegetable broth]], bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer partially covered until the beans are tender, about 45 minutes.
4. Scoop out about 1 cup of beans and broth, mash with a fork, and stir back in to thicken the soup.
5. Add the [[lacinato kale]] and cook until wilted and tender, about 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
6. Meanwhile, heat the remaining tablespoon of olive oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Add the remaining [[garlic]] slices and cook until golden and crispy, about 2 minutes. Drain on a paper towel.
7. Ladle soup into bowls, top with the crispy garlic, and finish with a drizzle of olive oil. Serve with [[crusty bread]].
