# Roasted Cauliflower with Tahini, Herbs, and Pistachios

## Ingredients

- cauliflower - 1 large head, cut into thick wedges through the core
- extra-virgin olive oil - 3 tbsp
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- tahini - 3 tbsp
- lemon - 1, juiced :: id=lemon
- garlic - 1 clove, finely grated
- warm water - 2 tbsp
- pistachios - 1/4 cup, roughly chopped
- fresh parsley - 1/4 cup, roughly chopped
- fresh mint - 2 tbsp, roughly chopped
- Aleppo pepper or red pepper flakes - 1/2 tsp
- flaky sea salt - for finishing

## Steps

1. Heat the oven to {425F}. Arrange the [[cauliflower]] wedges on a rimmed
   baking sheet. Drizzle with [[extra-virgin olive oil]] and season generously
   with [[kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper]].
2. Roast until deeply golden on the bottom and tender when pierced with a knife,
   25–30 minutes. Flip the wedges halfway through for even browning.
3. While the cauliflower roasts, whisk together the [[tahini]], [[lemon]] juice,
   [[garlic]], and [[warm water]] until smooth and pourable. Season with salt —
   it should be tangy and a little loose. Add more water if it seizes up.
4. In a small bowl, toss the [[pistachios]], [[fresh parsley]], [[fresh mint]],
   and [[aleppo pepper or red pepper flakes]] together.
5. Arrange the roasted cauliflower on a platter. Spoon the tahini sauce over and
   around the wedges.
6. Scatter the pistachio-herb mixture on top. Finish with [[flaky sea salt]] and
   an extra squeeze of [[lemon]] juice.

## Notes

Serves 4 as a side. The tahini sauce can be made a day ahead — it thickens in
the fridge, so thin it with a splash of water before serving. Broccoli works
as a substitute for the cauliflower.
