# Chicken Stir-Fry with Snap Peas and Cashews

## Ingredients

- boneless skinless chicken thighs - 1 lb, cut into 3/4-inch pieces :: id=chicken also=450g
- soy sauce - 2 tbsp :: also=30ml
- rice vinegar - 1 tbsp :: also=15ml
- cornstarch - 1 tsp
- peanut or vegetable oil - 2 tbsp :: id=oil also=30ml
- snap peas - 8 oz, strings removed :: also=225g
- red bell pepper - 1, cut into thin strips
- scallions - 3, sliced, whites and greens separated
- fresh ginger - 1 tbsp, minced
- garlic - 2 cloves, minced
- roasted cashews - 1/3 cup :: also=45g
- sesame oil - 1 tsp :: also=5ml
- cooked jasmine rice - for serving

## Steps

1. Toss the [[chicken]] with 1 tbsp of the [[soy sauce]], the [[rice vinegar]],
   and [[cornstarch]] in a bowl. Let sit 10 minutes.

2. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat until smoking. Add 1 tbsp of
   [[oil]] and swirl to coat. Add the [[chicken]] in a single layer and cook
   without stirring until golden on the bottom, about 2 minutes. Stir-fry until
   just cooked through, another 2 minutes. Transfer to a plate.

3. Add the remaining [[oil]] to the pan. Add the [[snap peas]] and [[red bell
   pepper]] and stir-fry until crisp-tender and lightly charred, about 3
   minutes.

4. Add the [[scallion whites -> scallions]], [[fresh ginger]], and [[garlic]].
   Stir-fry 30 seconds until fragrant.

5. Return the [[chicken]] to the pan. Add the remaining tablespoon of [[soy
   sauce]], the [[roasted cashews]], and [[sesame oil]]. Toss everything to
   combine.

6. Top with [[scallion greens -> scallions]] and serve immediately over
   [[cooked jasmine rice]].

## Notes

Substitute shrimp or extra-firm tofu for the chicken if you prefer. Broccoli
florets work well in place of the snap peas — just add a splash of water to
help them steam through.
