# Rigatoni with Slow-Roasted Tomato Sauce

## Ingredients

- roma tomatoes - 2 lbs, halved lengthwise :: id=tomatoes also=900g
- extra-virgin olive oil - 1/4 cup :: also=60ml
- garlic - 5 cloves, smashed
- red pepper flakes - 1/4 tsp
- sugar - 1/2 tsp
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- rigatoni - 1 lb :: also=450g
- fresh basil - 1/2 cup, torn :: id=basil also=15g
- Parmigiano-Reggiano - 2 oz, finely grated, plus more for serving :: also=60g

## Steps

1. Heat the oven to {300F}. Arrange the [[tomatoes]] cut-side up on a rimmed
   baking sheet. Drizzle with [[extra-virgin olive oil]], scatter the [[garlic]]
   cloves among them, and sprinkle with [[red pepper flakes]], [[sugar]], and a
   generous pinch of salt.

2. Roast until the tomatoes are deeply concentrated, jammy, and slightly
   caramelized at the edges, about 2 hours. They should look shrunken and
   intensely colored.

3. Transfer the roasted [[tomatoes]] and [[garlic]] to a wide bowl. Use the back
   of a fork to coarsely crush them into a chunky sauce — leave some texture.
   Season with salt and pepper.

4. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook the
   [[rigatoni]] until about 2 minutes short of al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta
   water before draining.

5. Add the drained pasta to the bowl of sauce along with 1/2 cup (120 ml) of the
   reserved pasta water. Toss vigorously until the sauce clings to the pasta,
   adding more pasta water a splash at a time if needed.

6. Fold in most of the [[basil]] and the [[parmigiano-reggiano]]. Toss once more,
   season with salt and pepper, and serve topped with the remaining basil and
   extra cheese.

## Notes

The long roast time is what makes this — it concentrates the tomatoes into
something much more flavorful than a quick sauté. The sauce comes together
while the pasta boils, so timing is easy. Leftovers keep well for 3 days.
