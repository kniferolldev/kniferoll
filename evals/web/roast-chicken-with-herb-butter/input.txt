<div class="headnote">
  <p>I've been making this roast chicken every Sunday for three years. It started as
  a way to use up herbs from my overgrown garden, and now it's the thing my family
  requests more than anything else. The butter goes under the skin and bastes the
  meat from the inside as it roasts — the result is incredibly juicy with crackling
  crisp skin.</p>
  <p>The key is to let the chicken air-dry in the fridge uncovered for at least a few
  hours, ideally overnight. Dry skin = crispy skin. Don't skip this.</p>
</div>

<div class="ad-inline"><!-- advertisement --></div>

<section id="tips">
  <h2>Tips for Success</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Use a 3.5–4 lb chicken. Larger birds take longer and the skin can overbrown.</li>
    <li>Room temperature butter blends much more easily with the herbs.</li>
    <li>Let the chicken rest before carving — at least 10 minutes.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="recipe">

<div class="recipe-card">

<h2>Roast Chicken with Herb Butter</h2>

<div class="recipe-meta">
  <span>Prep: 15 minutes (plus 2–24 hours drying time)</span>
  <span>Cook: 1 hour</span>
  <span>Serves: 4</span>
</div>

<h3>Ingredients</h3>
<ul>
  <li>1 whole chicken (3½ to 4 lbs)</li>
  <li>4 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature</li>
  <li>2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves</li>
  <li>1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped</li>
  <li>2 cloves garlic, minced</li>
  <li>1 teaspoon lemon zest</li>
  <li>1½ teaspoons kosher salt, divided</li>
  <li>½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper</li>
  <li>1 lemon, halved</li>
  <li>1 head of garlic, halved crosswise</li>
</ul>

<h3>Instructions</h3>
<ol>
  <li>The night before (or at least 2 hours ahead), pat the chicken dry with paper
  towels, inside and out. Set it on a rack over a baking sheet and refrigerate
  uncovered. This dries out the skin for maximum crispiness.</li>

  <li>When ready to cook, take the chicken out of the fridge 30 minutes before
  roasting. Preheat the oven to 425°F with a rack in the lower-middle position.</li>

  <li>Make the herb butter: mash together the softened butter, thyme, rosemary,
  minced garlic, lemon zest, 1 teaspoon of the salt, and the pepper until
  well combined.</li>

  <li>Loosen the skin over the breasts and thighs by gently sliding your fingers
  underneath it. Push about two-thirds of the herb butter under the skin and
  spread it over the meat. Rub the remaining butter all over the outside of
  the bird. Season the outside with the remaining ½ teaspoon salt.</li>

  <li>Stuff the cavity with the halved lemon and halved garlic head. Tie the legs
  together with kitchen twine if you like, or leave them loose for crispier thighs.</li>

  <li>Place the chicken breast-side up in a cast iron skillet or roasting pan.
  Roast for 50–60 minutes, until the skin is deep golden brown and the juices
  run clear when you pierce the thigh, or until a thermometer inserted in the
  thickest part of the thigh reads 165°F.</li>

  <li>Transfer to a cutting board and rest for 10–15 minutes before carving.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Notes</h3>
<p>The pan drippings are wonderful. After resting the chicken, tilt the skillet and
spoon off most of the fat, then add a splash of white wine or chicken stock and
scrape up the browned bits over medium heat for a quick pan sauce.</p>

<p>Leftovers keep well for 3 days. The carcass makes excellent stock.</p>
